### Five risk themes (ultra-short map)

1. **Autonomy** — A “country of geniuses in a datacenter” may not stay aligned; mitigations include Constitutional AI, interpretability, monitoring, and policy.
2. **Destructive misuse** — Mercenary-like capability lowers the bar for large-scale harm (e.g. biology); defenses combine model controls, screening, and R&D.
3. **Power-seeking misuse** — Dictators, states, or platforms could centralize decisive power; export controls, democratic guardrails, and corporate governance matter.
4. **Economic shock** — Even peaceful deployment could concentrate wealth and displace labor; Amodei argues for metrics, innovation-friendly policy, and redistribution.
5. **Indirect effects** — Surveillance, addiction-like use, and redefining work and meaning.

The piece argues for **decisive but careful** action—not stopping progress, but buying time on chip access to authoritarian states and treating the transition as a serious civilizational challenge.
